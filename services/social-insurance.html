Social Insurance Number (SIN)

Essential Information About Your Canadian Social Insurance Number

What is a SIN?

Purpose

  • Work in Canada
  • Access government programs
  • File taxes
  • Access banking services

Format

  • 9-digit number
  • Unique identifier
  • Cannot be used by others

Types

  • Permanent SIN (Citizens/PR)
  • Temporary SIN (Work Permits)
  • Expires with status

Who Needs a SIN?

Canadian Citizens

  • Born in Canada
  • Naturalized citizens

Permanent Residents

  • New immigrants
  • Protected persons

Temporary Residents

  • Work permit holders
  • Study permit holders with work authorization
  • Temporary resident permit holders

How to Apply

1

Gather Required Documents

  • Primary identity document
  • Status in Canada document
  • Secondary ID (if needed)
  • Support documents (name change, etc.)
2

Choose Application Method

  • In person at Service Canada Centre
  • By mail (if no Service Canada nearby)
  • Online application (if eligible)
3

Submit Application

  • Complete all required fields
  • Sign the application
  • Include all documents
4

Receive Your SIN

  • Immediate issue (in person)
  • Mail delivery (5-10 business days)
  • Keep your SIN secure

Protecting Your SIN

Do Share With

  • Employers
  • Tax authorities
  • Financial institutions
  • Government programs

Don't Share With

  • Landlords
  • Mobile phone companies
  • Educational institutions (except for tax forms)
  • Potential employers (before hiring)

Security Tips

  • Never carry your SIN card/letter
  • Store in secure location
  • Shred documents with SIN
  • Report lost/stolen SIN immediately

Important Notice

Your Social Insurance Number is confidential. Service Canada will never ask for your SIN by email or phone. Be cautious of scams and protect your identity.
