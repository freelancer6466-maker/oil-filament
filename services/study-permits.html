Study Permits

Your Gateway to Education in Canada

Study Permit Overview

A study permit is an official document issued by the Canadian government that allows foreign nationals to study at designated learning institutions (DLIs) in Canada.

Most international students need a study permit to study in Canada. You must apply for a study permit before coming to Canada.

Key Requirements

  • Acceptance letter from a designated learning institution (DLI)
  • Proof of financial support
  • Clean criminal record
  • Medical examination (if required)
  • Intent to leave Canada after studies
  • Valid passport or travel document
  • Language test results (if applicable)

Application Process

Step 1: Get Accepted

Receive an acceptance letter from a designated learning institution (DLI)

Step 2: Prepare Documents

Gather all required documents including financial proof

Step 3: Apply Online

Submit your application through the IRCC portal

Step 4: Biometrics

Provide biometrics if required

Step 5: Wait for Decision

Processing times vary by country

Working While Studying

With a valid study permit, you may be able to:

  • Work up to 20 hours per week during regular academic sessions
  • Work full-time during scheduled breaks
  • Work as a co-op student or intern if it's part of your program
